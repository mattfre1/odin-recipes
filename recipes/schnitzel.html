Schnitzel

Description

Schnitzel is a world wide meal. Almost every country has some kind of dish that is similiar or very close to schnitzel. Word schnitzel is German origin and most famous is Wiener Schnitzel.

Ingredients

  • 4 slices of pork or chicken breasts
  • 200 g of plain flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 30 ml milk
  • 200 g of breadcrumbs
  • salt
  • pepper

Steps

  1. Trim meat of any unwanted fat (if desired). Lay the pork or veal chops out on a countertop between two layers of plastic wrap. Pound out chops until 1 cm thick. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Spread flour out into a shallow dish. In another shallow dish, whisk eggs together with lemon juice. In a third shallow dish, spread out bread crumbs.
  3. Pour about 2 cm of vegetable oil into a large deep skillet. Heat the oil.
  4. Dredge chops into flour to coat, then immediately dip into eggs to coat, followed by bread crumbs to coat.
  5. Place chops into the hot oil and fry 3 to 4 minutes. Work in batches if needed based on size of pan. Chops shouldn't be crowded.
  6. Gently flip over and cook for an additional 3 to 4 minutes on the other side until is browned and crisp.
  7. Serve hot with lemon slices and your favourite side dish.
